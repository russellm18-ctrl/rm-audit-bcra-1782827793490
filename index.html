// Findings

  • THE STRUCTURAL METADATA COLLAPSE: Your project photography—which appears to be your primary proof-of-work—carries zero structural weight in search because your images are deployed as decorative files with no embedded alt-text, no JSON-LD schema identifying you as an ArchitecturalFirm, and no geo-tagged EXIF data tying your portfolio to Tacoma or Spokane. Right now, a developer or municipal procurement officer searching "architecture firm Tacoma WA civic projects" is landing on a competitor whose images are properly tagged and schema-marked, while your work is invisible to that query. Every portfolio image you've published is a dead asset—it renders on screen but contributes nothing to your search authority in either market.
  • THE HIDDEN TRUST DEFICIT: The public agency coordinator or institutional client evaluating BCRA for a community-scale project reads "for every community, for every future" and immediately needs to see the built work behind that claim—and your site delivers nothing specific. No named project, no location, no before/after, no occupancy photo of a real building serving a real community in Tacoma or Spokane. That buyer closes the tab because your written positioning is aspirational and your visual evidence is nonexistent, which registers as either an early-stage firm or one with something to hide.
  • THE COMPETITIVE EXPOSURE: A firm like Integrus Architecture, operating in your same Spokane market, publishes named civic and K-12 portfolio entries with exterior construction photography, interior occupancy imagery, and client-attributed project narratives—giving institutional procurement teams the visual due diligence package they require before shortlisting. You are offering a philosophy statement where a project record should be. That gap costs you shortlist inclusion on public contracts where visual project documentation is used to assess firm capability before a single phone call is made.
  • THE PHOTO-FIRST UPGRADE: Commission a structured asset capture across your completed Tacoma and Spokane projects—exterior, interior, occupancy, and construction-phase imagery—and deploy each image with a strict metadata protocol: descriptive alt-text naming the project type and city, JSON-LD ArchitecturalFirm and CreativeWork schema, and EXIF geodata matching the project location. Each image then functions as a searchable, schema-verified proof point that simultaneously builds geographic search authority and gives institutional clients the visual due diligence record they need to move you from unknown to shortlisted. This is not a photoshoot—it is a structured data and evidence deployment that converts your existing built work into a continuously compounding search and sales asset.
